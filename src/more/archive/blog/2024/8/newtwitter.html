If We Were the CEO of Twitter

August 16, 2024

Me and a friend decided to one day brainstorm a bunch of ideas for what we would do if we were the CEO of Twitter. This is what we came up with.

Additions

  • Sharing Tweets as an image - Nothing is going to stop people from screenshotting Tweets, so why not add a button in the share menu that generates a fancy image, with custom Twitter branding?
  • Before the most major desktop redesign, there was a row that showed the 10 most people who liked/retweeted a Tweet right next to the engagement counts. We want to bring this back
  • Third party integrations and embeds. For example, if someone posts a Spotify link, you can listen to a preview without leaving the timeline
  • Sorting Tweets on a profile or in a reply section. For example, sort by most liked, most retweeted, most recent
  • Searching and sorting your own likes (because of this, bookmarks are removed)
  • Mute, block, and report as spam, which reports as spam and blocks the user
  • When an account is deactivated, all of their Tweets remain, just no longer linked to that user. Identicons are used in profile pictures to tell deactivated users apart
  • Hashtags can now be followed, like on Instagram
  • Option to hide Retweets on the main feed and on profile pages
  • Option to only show Tweets with media attached to them in the timeline
  • You can pin searches to the timeline
  • View a list of websites/news articles that a Tweet has been embeded in
  • Achievement and badge system, for fun
  • Advanced profile customization, including a cosmetic shop, for Twitter Blue users
  • Relationship graph for Twitter Blue users (like the Obsidian content graph)
  • Advanced formatting options for Twitter Blue users such as text color, fonts, and BiU. Basically anything that Tumblr offers
  • You can hide the "Follows you" badge from other people or from your client. You cannot hide who you follow

Changes

  • The website is called Twitter again =)
  • The logo is now a rubber ducky
  • All terminology from before the X "rebrand" is restored, except the "Blaze your Glory" tagline is kept because its really funny
  • Image and video players are reworked so they don't suck anymore, as well as to support GIFs
  • Quotes and Replies are merged into one. Replies are the way to interact, and retweeting a reply shows it like a quote Tweet on the timeline, with additional context shown if needed
  • Replies from private accounts to public Tweets are now public
  • Advertising is rebranded to be more like Tumblr Blazing
  • Comments are just sorted by like count by default
  • Threads now remove any redundant, repeated user interface elements such as account name and username
  • Algorithm now displays reasons why something appeared on your timeline
  • Hashtags included at the end of Tweets are collapsed on the home timeline
  • Tweets now include the full timestamp
  • Replies that just mention people are now hidden except to the people mentioned. If someone replies to one of these mentions, an indicator such as "User replied to mentioner
  • Replies to a retweet are now made more evident that they are replies to a retweet
  • Notification and DM tabs are merged into one page
  • Likes are made public
  • "Instagram ahh" notification count bubble
  • Better notification feed UI for account notifications
  • Mutuals are now referred to as friends
  • Maximum image size is increased to 6000x6000
  • Longer videos are reduced to 10 minutes and anybody can upload them
  • Blue checks are reverted to how they worked before Elon Musk. They no longer have different colors to indicate different meanings
  • Media Studio is lumped into Tweetdeck and both are made free
  • Twitter Blue users get a last word grace period. For example, you can finish the last word of your Tweet, even if it goes over the character limit.
  • Twitter Blue is reworked to primarily be a beta testing program with cosmetic benefits and ad free
  • Content Warnings are reworked. You can now set to either completely hide Tweets with certain content warnings, put them behind an interstistial, or ignore the content warnings.

Removals

  • Communities (all existing community Tweets will be converted into regular Tweets)
  • Job Searching & related features
  • Grok
  • Bookmarks (as likes will now serve their purpose)
  • Community Notes (changes around the site will eliminate the need for them)
  • Polls (advertisers can still have access to them for ads)
  • Location Stamping (hashtags work)
  • Hidden Replies
  • Search bans
  • Extended character limit
  • Lists
  • Revenue sharing
  • Creator subscriptions
  • Articles
